Debra's Biography

Debra is a London based Actor and Director originally from Durham. To escape the boredom of growing up in a North East mining village Debra turned her hand to dancing from the age of 3, which led her to win National and International Titles. Her passion for dancing led her to complete a HND in Dance at Dance City in Newcastle and on to performing in numerous performances and musical at The Theatre Royal and Northern Stage; including 42nd Street and the stage debut of Face the Musical during which her natural talent for acting was discovered.

Debra went on to complete a BA Hons in Performing Arts and an MA in Acting at East 15 Acting School. Since 2005 Debra has worked on both stage and screen and some of her credits include: Rita (Educating Rita - Stage), Hayley (Isn’t Life a Funny Thing - Stage) Helen (Saving Grace - Film), La Gouloue (BBC Radio Production) and Lucy (Aracna - Film - Ch4).

Debra is also an experienced facilitator and workshop leader and works in partnership with many schools and organisations; including Durham County Council, Durham Constabulary, Croydon Youth Theatre, The Children’s University, One Punch UK, Share if you Care to create devised and ensemble theatre that explores social and political issues that have an impact on today’s society. She believes that theatre offers people a voice and it gives people a chance to share their own experiences with the audience, giving them a sense of self-worth and empowerment.

Debra is experienced in Physical Theatre, ensemble, Improvisation and Dance and has worked with prestigious companies and Directors, including Complicite (residency), Scene Productions (ensemble), Anthony Meindl (AMAW), Chiara D’anna (Commedia dell'arte), Glenn Tillin (ensemble).
